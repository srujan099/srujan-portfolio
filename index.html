From scattered spend to a clear winner.

A real Meta Ads diagnostic I ran at Pixis for Blissclub, a women's activewear eCommerce brand — turning 19 live adsets of noisy data into one prioritized call.

Meta Ads · eCommerce

Blissclub — Finding the Signal in 19 Live Adsets

The account was running 19 adsets across multiple campaigns with no clear read on which audience-creative combination was actually working — spend was getting spread thin instead of doubled down.

Visit Blissclub.com →
01

The problem

CTR, CVR, and CPL were all moving in different directions month to month, with no single adset or campaign clearly outperforming. Budget was getting allocated roughly evenly instead of toward what was actually converting.

02

What the data showed

Ranking every campaign and adset by ROAS surfaced two distinct winners: Campaign 5 was quietly driving 37% of all results on its targeting alone, while Campaign 7 posted the strongest CVR (4.55%) and ROAS (2.38) — signal that its creative, not its audience, was the differentiator.

03

The call I made

Rather than reallocating budget evenly across "good enough" performers, I recommended merging Campaign 5's proven targeting with Campaign 7's creative — concentrating spend on the one combination both signals agreed on, and pulling budget out of the bottom-quartile adsets entirely.
